Privacy Policy

Last updated: 7 July 2026

SnipClip is a macOS screenshot and annotation app developed by Luke Welch. This policy describes what data SnipClip does and does not collect.

Data we collect

Nothing. SnipClip collects no personal data, usage analytics, crash reports, or telemetry of any kind. The app does not connect to the internet during normal use.

Screenshots and images

Screenshots you capture stay entirely on your Mac. They are never uploaded, transmitted, or shared with us or any third party. SnipClip accesses your screen only when you explicitly trigger a capture.

Purchase information

If you purchase the unlock via the Mac App Store, that transaction is handled entirely by Apple. We receive no payment information, no email address, and no account details. Apple's privacy policy governs that transaction: apple.com/legal/privacy.

Free trial

The 24-hour free trial is tracked locally on your Mac using a timestamp stored in your app's preferences (UserDefaults). This information never leaves your device.

System permissions

SnipClip requests Screen Recording permission on first launch. This permission is required to capture your screen. You can revoke it at any time in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Screen Recording.

Children

SnipClip does not collect data from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a revised date at the top.

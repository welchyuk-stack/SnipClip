How do I take a screenshot?

Click the SnipClip icon in your menu bar and choose Capture Area, or press ⌘⇧2 from any app. Drag to select the area you want — your screenshot copies to clipboard the moment you release.

SnipClip is asking for Screen Recording permission

macOS requires this permission to capture your screen. Open System Settings → Privacy & Security → Screen Recording, find SnipClip, and toggle it on. You only need to do this once.

How do I use the markup tools?

After a capture the editor opens automatically. Choose a tool from the left sidebar — pen, arrow, rectangle, circle, highlight, or text — then draw directly on the image. Use the colour picker to change colour and the undo button to step back. Click Copy or press ⌘C to copy the annotated image.

How do I save my screenshot?

Click the save icon (↓) in the sidebar. Choose PNG or JPEG and pick a location. Alternatively, copy to clipboard with ⌘C and paste directly into any app.

My free trial has ended — how do I unlock?

When the paywall appears, click Unlock SnipClip to purchase for £1.29. If you've already purchased, click Restore Purchase and your unlock will be applied immediately.

I purchased but the app still shows the paywall

Open SnipClip, click the menu bar icon, and choose Capture Area. When the paywall appears click Restore Purchase. If that doesn't work, email us and we'll sort it.

Can I use SnipClip on multiple Macs?

Yes. Your purchase is tied to your Apple ID via the Mac App Store and can be used on any Mac signed into the same account. Click Restore Purchase on each Mac.

Does SnipClip capture passwords or private data?

SnipClip only captures what you explicitly select by dragging. Screenshots never leave your Mac — there is no cloud sync, no account, and no data collection of any kind. See our Privacy Policy.
